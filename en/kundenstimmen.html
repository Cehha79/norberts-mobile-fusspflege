How does a home visit work?

  • Appointments by arrangement
  • In your own home
  • Travel included

A home visit is wonderfully uncomplicated: no practice, no hunting for a parking space, no waiting room. I bring all the equipment with me – from the foot bath and the hygienically prepared instruments to the foot cream.

How your appointment goes

  1. Arrange an appointment by phone or WhatsApp
  2. I arrive on time and set up – a chair and a power socket are all I need
  3. A warm foot bath to start
  4. Care or massage, completely unhurried
  5. Finishing with a foot cream – payment is by cash, invoice or PayPal

Do I need to prepare anything?

No – you make yourself comfortable and I take care of the rest. I bring everything that's needed for the foot care with me.

All that helps is

  1. A comfortable seat with a little space in front of it
  2. A power socket nearby
  3. Your own towel if you prefer – not a must

Tip: It's best to take off socks and any foot jewellery before the appointment – that way all the time goes into the care itself.

How long does a foot care session take?

  • Professional foot care approx. 30 minutes
  • Massages: duration to follow

For the professional foot care, allow around 30 minutes – or a little more, depending on the condition of your feet. I never work against the clock: your feet get the time they need.

Foot care and massage combine beautifully in a single appointment – first the care, then the relaxation.

How do you ensure hygiene?

Hygiene is the foundation of any reputable foot care – and I make no compromises here. My professional background in care work shapes my approach to cleanliness to this day.

My firm rules

  1. Instruments are cleaned, disinfected and hygienically prepared after every use
  2. Disposable gloves for every session
  3. Fresh covers for every customer
  4. Tested, skin-friendly care products

Good to know: You are welcome to ask me about hygiene at any time – I'll gladly show you how the instruments are prepared.

Do you also care for people with diabetes?

With diabetes, cosmetic foot care is generally possible – as long as your feet are healthy. It's best to mention it briefly when booking your appointment; I will then work with particular care.

Important to know

With diabetic foot syndrome or other medical foot conditions, foot care belongs in a podiatry practice. Podiatric care can then be prescribed by a doctor – and in these cases the costs are often covered by the health insurance fund. Please speak to your GP about this.

My promise: I will tell you honestly whether I can help you – or which is the right place for you to turn to.

Cosmetic or podiatry? The comparison

How is this different from medical foot care?

In Germany, “podiatry” (Podologie) and “medical foot care” are legally protected titles: they require state training under the Podiatrists Act (PodG) and cover the treatment of medical foot conditions by state-certified podiatrists – often on a doctor's prescription.

What I offer is cosmetic foot care: the expert care of healthy feet – foot bath, nail and cuticle care, callus removal, wellness massages. The two fields complement each other, but neither replaces the other.
