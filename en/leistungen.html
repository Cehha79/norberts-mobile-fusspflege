Important to know

Cosmetic foot care – what does that mean?

I care for healthy feet. So that you know exactly where you stand, here is the clear distinction from medical foot care (podiatry).

What I take care of

Cosmetic care of healthy feet

  • Relaxing foot bath
  • Nails expertly trimmed and brought into shape
  • Cuticles gently pushed back and cared for
  • Gentle callus removal
  • Foot cream, foot massage and reflex-zone wellness massage

What belongs in podiatry

Medical foot care (podiatry) or a doctor's practice

  • Ingrown or severely thickened nails
  • Suspected nail or skin fungus
  • Warts and painful pressure points
  • Open sores and wounds
  • Diabetic foot syndrome

Why this clear distinction?

Germany's Podiatrists Act – briefly explained

In Germany, “podiatry” (Podologie) and “medical foot care” are legally protected titles (Podiatrists Act, PodG). They are reserved for state-certified professionals who treat medical foot conditions – often on a doctor's prescription. As a trained foot care specialist, I stand for the cosmetic care of healthy feet – the two fields complement each other, but neither replaces the other.

For me, this honesty is part of working carefully: if I notice anything during an appointment that should be looked at by a podiatrist or a doctor, I will tell you openly and point you to the right place to go. It goes without saying that I work with hygienically prepared instruments and tested care products – cleanliness and diligence are the foundation of all good care.
